## 1. Market Analysis

### Industry Overview
- **Market Size:** SA industrial rope & rigging market estimated at R2B+ annually
- **Growth:** 5-7% YoY driven by construction, mining, agriculture
- **Online Penetration:** Low (<10%), significant digital opportunity

### Competitive Landscape
| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| Major Distributors | Established, wide range | Poor online presence |
| Hardware Chains | Brand recognition | Limited specialty |
| Direct Importers | Price competitive | No local support |

### SWOT Analysis
| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----------|------------|
| Quality products | New brand awareness |
| E-commerce capability | Limited marketing budget |
| Local expertise | Small team |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|---------------|---------|
| Digital-first B2B | Price competition |
| Content authority | Economic uncertainty |
| Niche specialization | Supply chain issues |

## 2. Target Audience Segments

### Primary Segments

#### Segment 1: Industrial Buyers (B2B)
- **Profile:** Procurement managers, site managers, safety officers
- **Industries:** Construction, mining, manufacturing, logistics
- **Purchase Behavior:** Bulk orders, recurring needs, price-sensitive
- **Decision Factors:** Quality certifications, bulk pricing, credit terms
- **Channels:** LinkedIn, Google Search, Email

**Persona: "Procurement Pete"**
- 45 years old, 20+ years industry experience
- Values: Reliability, compliance, supplier relationships
- Pain Points: Lead times, quality consistency, documentation
- Content Needs: Product specs, certifications, bulk pricing

#### Segment 2: Trade Professionals (B2B/B2C)
- **Profile:** Contractors, riggers, farmers, boat owners
- **Industries:** Agriculture, marine, construction trades
- **Purchase Behavior:** Project-based, specific requirements
- **Decision Factors:** Technical specs, application advice, availability
- **Channels:** Google Search, YouTube, Facebook

**Persona: "Contractor Chris"**
- 35 years old, owns small construction/rigging business
- Values: Durability, quick delivery, expert advice
- Pain Points: Finding right product, wastage, storage
- Content Needs: How-to guides, product comparisons, safety info

#### Segment 3: DIY/Hobbyist (B2C)
- **Profile:** Home owners, craft enthusiasts, outdoor adventurers
- **Industries:** Home improvement, crafts, camping, sailing
- **Purchase Behavior:** Small quantities, diverse needs
- **Decision Factors:** Price, ease of purchase, reviews
- **Channels:** Google Search, Facebook, Instagram, Pinterest

**Persona: "DIY Diana"**
- 40 years old, homeowner, outdoor enthusiast
- Values: Convenience, clear instructions, fair pricing
- Pain Points: Not knowing what to buy, over-buying
- Content Needs: Project ideas, buying guides, tutorials

## 5. Email Marketing

### Email Program Structure

#### Transactional Emails (Automated)
1. **Order Confirmation** - Immediate
2. **Shipping Notification** - On dispatch
3. **Delivery Confirmation** - On delivery
4. **Invoice/Receipt** - With order confirmation
5. **Password Reset** - On request

#### Lifecycle Emails (Automated)
1. **Welcome Series** (3 emails over 7 days)
   - Email 1: Welcome + brand story
   - Email 2: Popular products + buying guide
   - Email 3: First purchase incentive

2. **Abandoned Cart** (3 emails)
   - Email 1: 1 hour - "Forgot something?"
   - Email 2: 24 hours - Product benefits
   - Email 3: 72 hours - Limited offer

3. **Post-Purchase** (3 emails)
   - Email 1: 3 days - Usage tips
   - Email 2: 14 days - Review request
   - Email 3: 30 days - Related products

4. **Win-Back** (2 emails)
   - Email 1: 60 days - "We miss you"
   - Email 2: 90 days - Special offer

#### Marketing Campaigns
1. **Monthly Newsletter**
   - New products
   - Featured content
   - Industry news
   - Customer spotlight

2. **Seasonal Campaigns**
   - End of year clearance
   - New year stock-up
   - Industry event tie-ins

3. **Promotional Campaigns**
   - Flash sales
   - Bundle offers
   - Volume discounts

### Email Segmentation Strategy

| Segment | Criteria | Content Focus |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| New Subscribers | < 30 days | Welcome, education |
| Active Customers | Purchased in 90 days | Loyalty, cross-sell |
| Lapsed Customers | No purchase 90+ days | Win-back, offers |
| High Value | LTV > R10,000 | VIP treatment, early access |
| B2B | Company email domain | Bulk pricing, credit terms |
| DIY | Gmail/personal email | Projects, tutorials |

## 6. SEO Strategy

#### Head Keywords (High Volume, High Competition)
- "rope south africa" (500 searches/mo)
- "ratchet straps" (1000 searches/mo)
- "cargo straps" (800 searches/mo)
- "lifting slings" (400 searches/mo)

#### Long-Tail Keywords (Lower Volume, Lower Competition)
- "polypropylene rope 10mm price" (100 searches/mo)
- "heavy duty ratchet straps for trucks" (200 searches/mo)
- "best rope for outdoor swing" (150 searches/mo)
- "cargo net for bakkie" (100 searches/mo)

#### Local Keywords
- "rope suppliers johannesburg"
- "buy rope cape town"
- "rigging equipment durban"

### On-Page SEO Checklist

For each product page:
- [ ] Unique title tag with product name + key feature
- [ ] Meta description with benefits + CTA
- [ ] H1 heading matching title
- [ ] Product schema markup (JSON-LD)
- [ ] Alt text on all images
- [ ] Internal links to related products/content
- [ ] Customer reviews with review schema

For each category page:
- [ ] Unique category description (200+ words)
- [ ] FAQ section with schema markup
- [ ] Filter-friendly URL structure
- [ ] Breadcrumb navigation
